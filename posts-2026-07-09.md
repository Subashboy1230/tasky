## Theme: Access is not adoption. Adoption is not learning.

The sacred cow: the AI-adoption number. Districts and vendors celebrate "X% of students have used AI" as if availability equals progress. This week at ISTE, even Microsoft's own recap led with "access is just the beginning." A Stanford study of two districts puts a hard number on the gap: 2.18 minutes of weekly use.

### LinkedIn draft (copy-paste ready)

2.18 minutes.

That is how long the average student used an AI tutor per week in a Stanford study of two districts.

Not per session. Per week.

Even with scheduled time to open the tool, only 61% of students in one district and 53% in the other used it at all. The platform's own bar for measurable reading gains is 30 minutes a week.

This week at ISTELive 2026 in Orlando, the loudest signal in Microsoft's recap was a quiet one. Their first takeaway: access to AI tools is just the beginning.

We keep scoring the wrong thing. Licenses bought. Logins created. Tools deployed.

None of those are learning.

A district can hit 100% access and still get two minutes of real use per kid. Access is the easy half. Engaged, structured, supported practice is the half that actually moves a reading score.

Access is not adoption. Adoption is not learning.

At the Greater Dayton School, the number we watched was never logins. It was the minutes a student actually spent in productive practice, and what they could do six weeks later.

The metric was never "students have the tool." It is whether the tool ever earns 30 real minutes of a kid's attention.
